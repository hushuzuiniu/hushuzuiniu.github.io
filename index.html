Biography

Hello! I’m Shu Hu, a Research Assistant at The University of Hong Kong, currently working under the guidance of Dr. Dhanasekaran Vijaykrishna (about our group). My current focus is on the genomic evolution and epidemiology of viruses like influenza and RSV, integrating genomic, clinical, and immunological data. This role at HKU, where I am also excited to begin my PhD studies in 2024, further develops my expertise in viral genomics, building upon my strong foundation in bioinformatics and genomic analysis from my Master’s degree at the University of Edinburgh. My professional journey has been marked by significant contributions at Shanghai Rightongene Technology Co. as a Bioinformatics Engineer, where I developed various analytical tools and pipelines. My projects include scRNA-seq analysis pipelines, RNA-seq clustering for leukemia molecular typing, and Python-based protein sequence analysis tools. My GitHub repositories (scRNA-seq analysis, RNA-seq clustering, Shiny tools, protein analysis) highlight my commitment to open science and collaboration.
